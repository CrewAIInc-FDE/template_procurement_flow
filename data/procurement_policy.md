# Corporate Procurement Policy (excerpt)

## Approval matrix

| Contract value (USD) | Approval required |
|---|---|
| Up to 150,000 | Procurement Manager (auto-approval eligible when sourced from approved suppliers) |
| 150,001 - 500,000 | Finance Director review + CFO sign-off |
| Above 500,000 | Executive committee |

## Requester limits

- Each employee has a personal request limit set by role (see HR master data). Requests exceeding the requester's limit require **human review** — they are not automatically rejected.
- Splitting a purchase into multiple smaller requests to stay under a limit ("order splitting") is a **policy violation**. Multiple requests by the same requester in the same category within 14 days must be assessed as one combined purchase.

## Category rules

- **IT & office equipment**: hardware requests from outside the IT department for more than 5 devices require IT endorsement. Standard-issue models from the catalog must be preferred over custom specs.
- **Safety gear / PPE**: only certified equipment (ANSI/EN or equivalent) may be purchased; supplier must document certification. PPE requests are considered high priority and should not be delayed by cost optimization alone.
- **Industrial spares**: treated as high-criticality equipment — supplier rules below apply strictly.
- **Services & software**: service or license contracts longer than 12 months require Legal review. Software must be sourced through existing vendor agreements when one covers the product.

## Sourcing requirements

- For **high-criticality equipment**, suppliers must hold **ISO 9001** (or equivalent) certification.
- Suppliers with an on-time delivery rate **below 90%** may not be awarded high-criticality contracts without a documented mitigation plan.
- Advance payments may not exceed **50%** of contract value. 100% advance payment is not permitted.
- Minimum warranty for high-criticality equipment: **12 months**.

## Conduct rules

- Purchases must serve a documented **business purpose**. Items for personal use, gifts, or rewards are not permissible through procurement (employee recognition runs through HR).
- Requested quantities must be plausibly justified by team size or operational need.

## Evaluation guidance

- Evaluate on **total cost of ownership** (price, warranty coverage, maintenance terms, payment terms, logistics), not unit price alone.
- Where offers are within **5% on total cost of ownership**, preference should be given to **local suppliers** to reduce lead times for spares and support regional development.
- Urgent requests may justify paying a premium for shorter lead times; the trade-off must be stated.
